Delhi Tour

Delhi, the vibrant capital of India, beckons travelers with its rich tapestry of history and modernity. From the majestic Red Fort to bustling markets like Chandni Chowk,
Delhi offers a captivating journey through time and culture.

Red Fort

The Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, India, stands as a symbol of Mughal architectural grandeur. Constructed by Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, its distinctive red sandstone walls enclose a majestic complex that served as the main residence of Mughal emperors for centuries.
Shah-jahaan 1700
Fort

Jama Masjid

Jama Masjid, located in Old Delhi, is one of the largest and most iconic mosques in India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century. Its grand architecture, adorned with red sandstone and white marble, attracts visitors with its impressive courtyard and three imposing domes.
Shah-jahaan 1650-1655
Mosque

Lotus temple

The Lotus Temple, located in Delhi, is a striking Bahá'í House of Worship known for its unique lotus flower-inspired architecture. Embracing the principles of unity and peace, it welcomes people of all faiths to meditate and pray in its serene and awe-inspiring surroundings.
Indian Government 1986
Temple

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar, an iconic UNESCO World Heritage Site in Delhi, stands tall as a masterpiece of Indo-Islamic architecture. it narrates a fascinating tale of India's rich historical heritage through its intricate carvings and towering presence.
Qutab-ud-din Aibak 1193
Monument

Akshar-Dham

Akshardham Temple, situated in Delhi, is a marvel of modern Indian architecture and spirituality. Completed in 2005, it boasts intricately carved sandstone, a stunning central monument, and a captivating cultural exhibition, making it a prominent cultural and religious landmark.
Indian Government 6 Nov 2005
Temple

Humayun Tomb

Humayun's Tomb, located in Delhi, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a splendid example of Mughal architecture, built in the 16th century. The tomb's symmetrical design, Persian influence, and lush surrounding gardens make it a serene and historically significant monument.
Mughal Emperor 1570
Tomb

India Gate

India Gate is an iconic war memorial in New Delhi, India, built in honor of the soldiers who sacrificed their lives in World War I. Its majestic arch and eternal flame serve as a solemn tribute to the nation's military heritage.
Edwin Lutyens(british) 1931
Memorial

Chandani Chowk

Chandni Chowk, one of the oldest and busiest markets in Old Delhi, is a vibrant tapestry of narrow lanes, bustling bazaars, and historical landmarks. Known for its diverse street food, traditional shops, and the Red Fort at one end, it offers a rich blend of history, culture, and commerce.
Mughal Emperor 1650
